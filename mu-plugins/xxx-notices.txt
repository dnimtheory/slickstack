When possible, always load tracking scripts in your site footer, not the header, for better performance and loading speeds. Using a WordPress plugin to do this is usually best, because you can also choose to not track certain users (e.g. Admins or logged-in customers) to avoid inaccurate statistics. Using GTM also might allow your company to better follow legal compliance rules such as the GDPR in Europe. Always keep in mind that every script, and every tracking feature that you add will cause your site to slow down more, so minimizing tracking is a good idea.</p>
    <p><em>This message will disappear automatically after a few days, thank you. â€” SlickStack</em></p>
    </div>
